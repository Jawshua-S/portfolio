Featured Project: Software & Procedural Onboarding

The Problem: Customer support onboarding at a fast-growing tech company was taking too long, leading to inconsistent ticket prioritization and premature manager escalations.

The Solution: Designed a highly polished, user-centered digital learning experience simulating a proprietary CRM workspace to train new hires on independent problem-solving and procedural compliance.

The Design Strategy: Focused heavily on LXD/UX design principles by chunking dense data tables into scannable cards and using scenario-based knowledge checks that test contextual critical thinking rather than rote memorization.

Tools Used: Apple Keynote (Advanced Vector Layout & UI Prototyping), Figma.

Core Instructional Design Decisions

  • Scenario-Based Assessment: Implemented critical thinking checkpoints (such as evaluating a single-user ticket under immediate 10-minute time constraints) to teach reps how to prioritize by business consequence and revenue impact rather than sheer user volume.
  • Cognitive Load Reduction: Transformed complex, technical CRM tables into visually isolated cards and clear color-coded statuses (High/Medium/Low) to decrease information fatigue during onboarding.
  • Constructive Feedback Loops: Programmed deep, analytical feedback layers that explain why specific choices are incorrect, utilizing errors as active learning opportunities.
