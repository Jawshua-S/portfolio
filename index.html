Customer De-escalation Simulation

The Problem: Customer support representatives often handle emotionally challenging situations where communication choices directly impact customer satisfaction and retention. New representatives may understand troubleshooting steps but struggle with empathy, ownership, and choosing effective responses during difficult interactions.

The Solution: Designed an interactive branching scenario that places learners in a realistic customer support conversation. Learners make decisions, receive immediate coaching feedback, and observe how their communication choices influence customer emotions and the final resolution outcome.

The Design Strategy: Applied scenario-based learning and branching logic to create a realistic practice environment where learners can safely make decisions and experience consequences. Used emotional feedback, coaching layers, and progressive decision points to reinforce customer service behaviors and improve judgment.

Tools Used: Articulate Storyline, Figma, Apple Keynote (UI Prototyping).

Core Instructional Design Decisions

  • Branching Scenario Design: Created a decision-based simulation where learner choices influence customer emotions, trust, and the direction of the interaction.
  • Immediate Feedback Loops: Developed coaching layers that explain why responses were effective or ineffective, transforming mistakes into learning opportunities.
  • Emotional Consequence Mapping: Designed customer sentiment changes throughout the scenario to demonstrate how communication decisions impact customer relationships over time.

Customer Support Escalation & Decision-Making Job Aid

The Problem: Customer support representatives needed a consistent way to evaluate incoming tickets and determine the appropriate response. New hires struggled with prioritization decisions, leading to unnecessary escalations, inconsistent handling, and longer resolution times.

The Solution: Designed an interactive escalation decision guide that simulates a customer support workflow. The experience guides learners through realistic scenarios, helps them identify priority levels, determine when escalation is required, and practice making independent decisions.

The Design Strategy: Applied instructional design and user experience principles by breaking complex decision-making processes into clear, manageable steps. Used scenario-based learning, visual hierarchy, and immediate feedback to reduce cognitive load and reinforce critical thinking skills.

Tools Used: Articulate Storyline, Figma, Apple Keynote (UI Prototyping).

Core Instructional Design Decisions

  • Scenario-Based Assessment: Designed decision-making scenarios that require learners to evaluate support tickets, identify priorities, and determine appropriate escalation paths based on business impact rather than ticket volume alone.
  • Cognitive Load Reduction: Simplified complex CRM information by organizing data into visually distinct cards, clear priority indicators, and structured workflows to reduce information overload during onboarding.
  • Constructive Feedback Loops: Developed feedback layers that explain the reasoning behind correct and incorrect decisions, turning mistakes into opportunities for deeper learning and improved judgment.
