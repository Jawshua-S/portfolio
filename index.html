Customer De-escalation Simulation

My Role: Designed and built end-to-end from scratch — instructional design, scenario writing, branching logic, UI design, and Storyline development, with no template base.

The Problem: New support reps often know the technical fix but freeze on the human side of a hard conversation — they don't know how to acknowledge frustration, take ownership of a mistake, or de-escalate before a customer disengages entirely.

The Solution: A branching scenario that drops learners into a live-feeling support chat. Every response they choose shifts the customer's emotional state — visualized through a real-time sentiment indicator — and changes which path the conversation takes next.

The Design Strategy: The core mechanic is consequence, not correctness. Instead of flagging "right" and "wrong" answers, the simulation shows learners the emotional ripple effect of their choice in the moment, then layers in coaching feedback explaining the reasoning — so the lesson lands as cause-and-effect, not a quiz grade.

Evaluation Plan: Success would be measured through scenario completion rate, decision accuracy across branch points, and a post-scenario confidence survey asking learners to rate their readiness to handle a similar real conversation.

Tools Used: Articulate Storyline, Figma, Apple Keynote (UI prototyping).

Core Instructional Design Decisions

  • Branching Scenario Design: Learner choices drive both the customer's emotional trajectory and the structural path of the conversation, so no two playthroughs feel identical.
  • Immediate Feedback Loops: Coaching layers surface right after each decision, explaining why a response worked or backfired while the moment is still fresh.
  • Emotional Consequence Mapping: A visible sentiment state (Frustrated → Calm → Happy) makes the abstract idea of "de-escalation" tangible and trackable across the scenario.

Customer Support Escalation & Decision-Making Job Aid

My Role: Instructional design, workflow mapping, UI design, and Storyline development.

The Problem: New hires had the knowledge to triage tickets correctly but no consistent framework for applying it — so escalation decisions varied rep to rep, driving up resolution times and unnecessary handoffs.

The Solution: An interactive decision guide that mirrors the actual CRM triage workflow, walking learners through realistic tickets and asking them to independently judge priority and escalation path — not just recall a policy.

The Design Strategy: The build strips a messy, multi-variable judgment call down into a clear decision tree. Ticket data is organized into scannable cards with visual priority cues, so learners practice pattern-recognition under the same time pressure and information density they'll face on the job.

Evaluation Plan: Success would be measured through triage accuracy against a scored answer key, time-to-decision per ticket, and a manager follow-up rating of on-the-job escalation consistency 30 days post-training.

Tools Used: Articulate Storyline, Figma, Apple Keynote (UI prototyping).

Core Instructional Design Decisions

  • Scenario-Based Assessment: Tickets are evaluated on business impact, not just volume or category — forcing learners to weigh context the way an experienced rep would.
  • Cognitive Load Reduction: CRM data is restructured into visually distinct cards with clear priority indicators, cutting the information overload that trips up new hires.
  • Constructive Feedback Loops: Feedback explains the reasoning behind each right or wrong call, so mistakes build judgment instead of just getting corrected.
